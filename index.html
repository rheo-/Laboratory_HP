Welcome to Iino Laboratory!

Iino Laboratory belongs to the Research Center for Information Innovation Research at the Future Industrial Technology Research Institute of the Tokyo Institute of Technology Science and Technology Creation Research Institute and is working on materials and devices related to information technology.

The main focal topics of this laboratory are organic semiconductors that can be utilized for fabricating large-area semiconductor thin films by a simple film-forming process for imaging devices and eletronic devices used for information input / output, mainly on technologies relating to images.

Among organic semiconductor materials, we focus on "liquid crystalline organic semiconductors", we are conducting research on material development, physical properties and electrical characteristics evaluation, process development, device application, and utilizing the printing process. We aim to develop imaging devices such as flexible and lightweight displays, light emitting devices, photoelectric conversion devices, etc.

In this laboratory, in order to consistently advance the development of materials, evaluation of fundamental physical properties and device application within the research group, students and researchers with different backgrounds such as electrical, chemical, physical, etc. cooperate and conduct research as a single team. Students belonging to this laboratory belong to the Electrical and Electronic Engineering Course of the Graduate School of Engineering and the Department of Creative Engineering of Physics and Electronic Systems, Graduate School of Comprehensive Science and Engineering. Therefore there are ample opportunities for students of chemistry / applied chemistry and organic material systems to join Iino Laboratory and pursue research in the field of organic semiconductor materials.

Keywords: Imaging devices, liquid crystalline organic semiconductors, TFT (thin film transistors), light sensors, imaging devices, flexible electronics, EL element, solar cells
