Welcome to Iino Laboratory!

Iino Laboratory belongs to the Research Center for Information Innovation Research at the Future Industrial Technology Research Institute of the Tokyo Institute of Technology Science and Technology Creation Research Institute and is working on materials and devices related to information technology.

The main focal topics of this laboratory are organic semiconductors that can be utilized for fabricating large-area thin films by simple film-forming processes for fabrication of eletronic devices. These devices are intended to be used for information input/output, mainly on technologies related to images.

Among organic semiconductor materials, Iino Laboratory focuses on the fabrication and utilization of "liquid crystalline organic semiconductors". Currently the members of this laboratory are conducting research on material development, evaluation of physical properties and electrical characteristics, process development, device application, and utilization of the printing process. This laboratory aims to develop innovative eletronic devices such as flexible and lightweight displays, light emitting devices, photoelectric conversion devices, etc.

In this laboratory, the objective is to consistently advance the development of materials, evaluate the fundamental physical properties and practical device applications. With this goal in mind, students and researchers with different backgrounds such as electrical engineering, chemitry, physics etc. cooperate and conduct research as a single team. Students affiliated to this laboratory belong to the Electrical and Electronic Engineering Course of the Graduate School of Engineering and the Department of Creative Engineering of Physics and Electronic Systems, Graduate School of Comprehensive Science and Engineering. Therefore there are ample opportunities for students of chemistry / applied chemistry and organic material systems to join Iino Laboratory and pursue research in the field of organic semiconductor materials.

Keywords: Imaging devices, liquid crystalline organic semiconductors, TFT (thin film transistors), light sensors, imaging devices, flexible electronics, EL element, solar cells
